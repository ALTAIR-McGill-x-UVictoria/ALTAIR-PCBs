The power connector is a 4-pin (1x4) header. Pin 1 (closest to middle of the board) is battery voltage, pin 2 is analog/main ground, pin 3 is 3.3V DVDD for the oscillator and ADC, and pin 4 is "digital ground," used by the IVC102 integrator as a separate ground before connecting to analog ground across a 0-Ohm jumper.

The system header has 1 row of 20 pins, with pin 1 closest to the power header.

1. INT_SLCTA (ACF2101 select channel A)
2. SYS_RESET (active low, reset the ADCs on both Sergeant and Soldier)
3. INT_RST (ACF2101 reset switch)
4. INT_HLD (ACF2101 hold switch)
5. INT_SLCTB (ACF2101 select channel B)
6. DRDY1 (active low, an output from one board's ADC)
7. DRDY2 (active low, an output from the other board's ADC)
8. SPI_MOSI
9. STRT1 (an input on one board's ADC)
10. STRT2 (an input on the other board's ADC)
11. SPI_MISO
12. SPI_CS3 (one board's reverse-biasing DAC)
13. SPI_CS4 (the other board's DAC)
14. SPI_SCLK
15. SPI_CS1 (one board's ADC)
16. SPI_CS2 (the other board's ADC)
17. IVC_SW_1_Sol (Soldier IVC102 switch 1)
18. IVC_SW_2_Sol (Soldier IVC102 switch 2)
19. IVC_SW_1 (Sergeant IVC102 switch 1)
20. IVC_SW_2 (Sergeant IVC102 switch 2)
The header also has grounded mounting pins. I'm not sure how specialized the connector that plugs into this header is though.
